10 Century Blvd.

Nashville, TN 37214

We are organizing a workshop on February 19th and 20th, 1996 to discuss the the formation of a form for parallel and sparse BLAS. This will be a follow on to the discussions that took place On November 13th and 14th, 52 people attended a Workshop on Parallel BLAS and Related Interfaces at the Univertsity of Tennessee and the birds of a feather session that took place at the Supercomputer 95 meting in San Diego in December.

The existing BLAS have proven to be very effective in assisting portable, efficient software for sequential and some of the current class of high-performance computers. We would like to investigate the possibility of extending these standards to provide greater coverage of sparse matrices and provide additional facilities for parallel computing.

The purpose of this meeting is to review the findings of the Workshop in November and the BOF at the Supercomputer 95 confernece and to begin a forum to develop calling sequences for a set of low-level computational kernels for the parallel and sequential settings, as well as standards for basic matrix data structures, both dense and sparse.
